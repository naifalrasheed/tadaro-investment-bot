Technical Analysis Calculation Methodology

The technical score is calculated using a comprehensive analysis of multiple factors:

Component Calculation Method Weight
Sentiment Analysis Machine learning prediction of price direction based on pattern recognition 20%
Trend Analysis Short-term trend (5-20 days): ±10 points
Medium-term trend (20-50 days): ±15 points
Long-term trend (50+ days): ±5 points 		30%
Technical Indicators RSI (Oversold/Overbought): ±5 points
Moving average crossovers: ±10 points

Volume Analysis Recent volume changes relative to historical average 10%
Volatility Assessment Historical volatility compared to market and sector averages 5%
Price Pattern Recognition ML model identifies common patterns (double bottom, head & shoulders, etc.) 10%
Score Interpretation:
  • 75-100: Strong Buy - Excellent technical setup with multiple bullish signals
  • 50-75: Buy - Positive technical indicators suggest favorable entry point
  • 25-50: Hold - Mixed signals or transition period, monitor closely
  • 0-25: Sell - Multiple bearish indicators suggest avoiding entry or reducing position
Timeframe Considerations:

Technical scores represent different timeframes: Recent Performance indicators (1-3 months) may sometimes conflict with longer-term indicators. This is normal in markets and reflects short-term volatility within longer-term trends.

Naif Al-Rasheed Model Context:

In the Naif Al-Rasheed investment model, technical analysis is complementary to fundamental criteria (ROTC > 15%, P/E < 25, etc.). Strong technicals combined with solid fundamentals represent the most favorable investment candidates.
